## Structural Diagnosis

* The first clean joint is the **three-stage law**: platforms begin by subsidizing users, then shift surplus to business customers, then extract from both for shareholders.
* The next clean seam is the **case cascade**: Amazon and Facebook are not side examples; they are structural proofs of the same lifecycle.
* A hidden mechanism under the middle is **hostage architecture**: lock-in binds users to each other and sellers to audiences, so the platform can later degrade service without immediate exit.
* Another hidden mechanism is **algorithmic opacity as rent extraction**: ranking, recommendation, and moderation become adjustable valves for reallocating attention without transparent rules.
* The difficult seam is the **TikTok transition point**: the article argues TikTok has crossed from good-faith recommendation into selective “heating,” but it is still in the delicate early monetization phase rather than full terminal decay.
* The final clean seam is the **policy turn**: Doctorow stops diagnosing platform rot as a moral failure and reframes it as a structural governance problem requiring interoperability, end-to-end delivery, and freedom of exit.
