## Structural Diagnosis

* The first clean joint is the **problem statement seam**: the article begins with the empirical anomaly that Black Americans used Twitter at disproportionately high rates, forcing a reconsideration of how the platform is understood.
* The next clean seam is the **methodological split**: Brock refuses both neutral-interface assumptions and purely quantitative social-network readings, pairing interface analysis with discourse analysis.
* A hidden mechanism under the middle is **technocultural mediation**: Twitter’s brevity, mobile reach, hashtag function, and publicness do not just transmit culture; they shape how Black discourse becomes legible, visible, and misread.
* Another hidden mechanism is **racial framing asymmetry**: Black participation becomes marked as racial and culturally specific, while white participation remains unmarked, normal, and universal.
* The difficult seam is the **double reading of Black Twitter**: the same platform practices are interpreted either as civic/public discourse or as evidence of Black deficiency, depending on the racial affiliation and ideology of the observer.
* The final clean seam is the **publicness qualification**: Black Twitter is not “all Black online life,” but a publicly recognized subgroup whose visibility emerged through hashtags and trending topics, then may recede back into Black audiences.
