## The Shield and the Crowned Heaven

# The Shield and the Crowned Heaven

First of all he formed a shield, both large and solid, adorning it all over.

Around it he threw a shining border, triple, glittering, and a silver belt.

Five folds made the shield itself, and on it he formed many ornaments with cunning skill.

> On it he wrought the earth, and the heaven, and the sea.

He wrought the unwearied sun, the full moon, and all constellations.

The Pleiades and the Hyades were there, and the might of Orion.

The Bear, also called the Wain, revolves there and watches Orion.

The Bear alone is without a share of the baths of the ocean.

---

Within the shield, cities, armies, field, vineyard, oxen, dance, and Oceanus wait.

Gold, tin, silver, brass, blood, grapes, and river-light become one moving world.

## The City of Marriage, Feast, and Judgment

# The City of Marriage, Feast, and Judgment

On it also he fashioned two beautiful cities of articulate-speaking men.

In the one were marriages and feasts, bright under heaven like constellations.

They conducted the brides from their chambers through the city with brilliant torches.

Many a bridal song was raised, while young men danced in a circle.

Among them flutes and lyres gave forth their sounds beside the portals.

The women standing, each at her portals, admired the dance and the city.

---

But the people were assembled in the forum, for there a strife had arisen.

Two men strove for the ransom-money of a slain man.

One affirmed that he had paid all, but the other denied that he had received any thing.

Both wished to find a termination of the dispute before a judge.

The heralds reined in the people, and the elders sat on polished stones.

They sat in a sacred circle and held the staves of the loud-shouting heralds.

There lay in the midst two talents of gold for the judgment most correctly pronounced.

Even here, the shield holds Mars, Fate, gold, and law in the same bright border.

## The Besieged City, Ambush, and Battle

# The Besieged City, Ambush, and Battle

Around the other city sat two armies of people, glittering in arms.

A plan was agreeable to them: destroy it entirely or divide all the wealth in two.

The pleasant city did not yet obey, but was secretly arming for an ambush.

Beloved wives and young children stood upon the wall guarding it.

With them stood the men whom old age possessed.

---

Mars and Pallas Minerva led them, both golden and clad in golden garments.

They reached a place fit to lay an ambush, by a river and watering-place.

There they settled, wrapped in shining brass, while two spies sat apart.

The spies watched for sheep and curvated oxen moving toward the river.

Two shepherds followed, amusing themselves with pipes, not foreseeing fraud.

The ambushers slaughtered the herds of oxen and the fair flocks of sheep.

They slew the shepherds besides, and the tumult among the oxen reached the councils.

The besiegers mounted nimble-footed horses and pursued them to the banks of the river.

There they fought a battle and wounded one another with brazen spears.

Among them mingled Strife and Tumult, and destructive Fate.

Fate dragged one dead by the feet, and her garment was crimsoned with blood.

They turned like living mortals, fought, and drew away slaughtered bodies.

## The Fallow Field, Corn, Reapers, and Feast

# The Fallow Field, Corn, Reapers, and Feast

On it he placed a soft fallow field, rich glebe, wide, thrice-ploughed.

Many ploughmen drove hither and thither, turning round their teams.

When they reached the end of the field, a man gave them very sweet wine.

They turned themselves in series, eager to reach the other end of the deep fallow.

The field grew black behind, and was like ploughed land, though being golden.

This was a very great marvel within the shield.

---

There he also placed a field of deep corn, where reapers were reaping.

Sharp sickles were in their hands, and handfuls fell thick along the furrow.

Binders of sheaves tied the corn with straw ropes in series.

Boys gathered the handfuls and continually supplied the binders.

Among them the king in silence stood over a furrow, rejoicing in his heart.

Apostles apart, beneath an oak, prepared a feast after sacrificing a huge ox.

Women strewed much white barley meal as a dinner for the reapers.

The fallow field answers the forum, the river, the oxen, and the talents of gold.

- **fallow field**: The fallow field shows labor making black earth inside golden material.
- **ploughmen**: The ploughmen move in repeated turns, making agricultural rhythm visible.
- **sweet wine**: Sweet wine rewards labor and foretells the vineyard's grape-bearing abundance.
- **corn**: Corn is the second agricultural field, where cutting, binding, and feeding are ordered.
- **reapers**: The reapers translate ripe field into sheaves, watched by the silent king.
- **king**: The king stands in silence and rejoices over ordered harvest.
- **oak**: The oak marks the place where harvest becomes sacrificial meal and communal feeding.

## The Vineyard and the Linus Song

# The Vineyard and the Linus Song

On it also he placed a vineyard heavily laden with grapes, beautiful, golden.

The clusters throughout were black, and it was supported by silver poles.

Around it he drew a dark-blue trench, and around it a hedge of tin.

There was but one path by which the gatherers went to collect the vintage.

Young virgins and youths of tender minds carried sweet fruit in woven baskets.

In the midst of them a boy played pleasantly on a shrill lyre.

He sang the beautiful Linus song with a slender voice.

They followed with singing and shouting, beating the ground, hopping with their feet.

---

The vineyard repeats the dance, the bridal song, the gold, the tin, and the shield.

Its black grapes echo the black fallow field, blood-dark battle, and Oceanus border.

- **vineyard**: The vineyard turns agricultural abundance into music, path, basket, and vintage.
- **grapes**: The grapes are golden and black, joining beauty, darkness, wine, and harvest.
- **Linus song**: The Linus song carries the vineyard into ritual music and patterned movement.

## The Herds, Lions, Dogs, and Sheepfolds

# The Herds, Lions, Dogs, and Sheepfolds

On it he fashioned a herd of lofty-horned oxen, made of gold and tin.

Lowing, the oxen rushed from the stall to the pasture beside a murmuring river.

They moved along the waving reed, followed by four golden herdsmen.

Nine dogs, swift of foot, followed the oxen into the pasture.

Two terrible lions among the foremost oxen seized a bellowing bull.

The bull, dragging, roared loudly, while dogs and young men ran up to him.

The lions tore the hide of the huge ox and devoured entrails and black blood.

The shepherds vainly pressed upon them, urging on the fleet dogs.

The dogs refused to bite the lions, but stood near, barking and flying them.

---

On it also the illustrious Vulcan formed a large pasture of white sheep.

The sheep stood in a beautiful grove, with folds, covered sheds, and cottages.

Here the river of ambush returns as pasture, and Fate returns as lions.

### Links

- **oxen**: Oxen move through ploughing, ambush, sacrifice, and predation across the shield.
- **sheep**: Sheep link the ambush slaughter to the later peaceful sheepfolds.
- **lions**: The lions are the pastoral image of predation, echoing war's destructive force.

## The Dance of Daedalus and the Border of Oceanus

# The Dance of Daedalus and the Border of Oceanus

On it also the illustrious Vulcan embellished a dance.

It was like the dance in wide Gnossus that Daedalus composed for Ariadne.

There youths and alluring virgins danced with hands on each other's wrists.

The virgins had fine linen robes, and the youths wore well-woven tunics.

The youths shone slightly with oil and carried golden swords from silver belts.

At one time they moved lightly with skilled feet, like a potter testing a wheel.

At another time they moved in series towards each other.

A large crowd stood around the enjoyable dance, delighted.

Among them two tumblers, beginning their song, spun round in the midst.

---

In it he placed the vast strength of the river Oceanus.

Oceanus ran around the extreme border of the well-formed shield.

The shield closes where it began: circle, silver belt, dance, river, heaven, sea.

- **Ariadne**: Ariadne anchors the dance in mythic memory and composed movement.
- **dance**: The dance gathers the shield's circles, songs, crowds, and ordered motion.
