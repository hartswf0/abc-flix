## The Cosmos

# The Forging of the Shield

First of all Vulcan formed a shield, both large and solid,

adorning it all over with gold and precious materials;

and around it he threw a shining border, triple, glittering;

and from it suspended a shining silver belt.

Five were the folds of the shield itself;

but on it he formed many ornaments with cunning skill.

On it he wrought the earth, and the heaven, and the sea,

the unwearied sun, and the full moon;

## The Constellations

on it also, all the constellations with which the heaven is crowned,

- the Pleiades

- the Hyades

- the might of Orion

- and the Bear

which they also call by the appellation of the Wain,

which there revolves, and watches Orion;

but it alone is without a share of the baths of the ocean.

## The City of Peace

# The Two Beautiful Cities

On it also he fashioned two beautiful cities of articulate men.

In the one, indeed, were marriages and feasts;

and they were conducting the brides from their chambers

with brilliant torches, and many a bridal song was raised.

The young men were beginning a dance in a circle,

and among them flutes and lyres gave forth their sounds;

and the women standing, each at her portals, admired.

---

## The Forum

But the people were assembled in the forum,

for there a strife had arisen,

and two men strove for the ransom-money of a slain man;

both wished to find a termination before a judge.

> The people shouted to both, supporters on either side;

but the elders sat upon polished stones in a sacred circle,

and held in their hands the staves of the heralds;

with these then they arose, and adjudged in turn.

But there lay in the midst two talents of gold,

to give to him who should pronounce a judgment correctly.

## The City of War

# The City Besieged

But around the other beautiful city sat two armies of people,

glittering in arms; and a plan was agreeable to them,

either to destroy it entirely, or to divide the wealth.

But they did not yet obey, secretly arming for an ambush;

their beloved wives and young children stood upon the wall.

But they advanced; and Mars and Pallas Minerva led them,

both golden, and clad in garments of gold, beautiful and large.

---

## The Ambush by the River

But when they reached a place fit to lay an ambush, by a river,

where was a watering-place, they settled in shining brass.

Two spies watched when they might perceive the sheep and oxen.

These soon advanced, and two shepherds followed,

amusing themselves, for they did not foresee the fraud.

The armies ran up, and slaughtered the herds of oxen and sheep.

---

## The Battle

Then, having formed a line, they fought a battle by the river,

and wounded one another with their brazen spears.

Among them mingled Strife and Tumult, and destructive Fate,

and the garment around her shoulders was crimsoned with blood.

## The Fields and Vineyard

# The Fallow Field

On it he also placed a soft fallow field, thrice-ploughed;

and in it many ploughmen drove hither and thither.

When they reached the end, a man gave them sweet wine;

but it grew black behind, like unto ploughed land,

though being of solid gold; which was a very great marvel.

---

## The Harvest

There he also placed a field of deep corn, where reapers worked.

Some handfuls fell thick in series upon the ground,

and others the binders of sheaves were tying with straw ropes.

And among them the king in silence was standing, rejoicing.

Beneath an oak, they had sacrificed a huge ox for the feast.

---

## The Vineyard

On it also he placed a vineyard heavily laden with grapes,

beautiful, golden; but the clusters throughout were black.

Around it he drew a dark-blue trench, and a hedge of tin.

Young virgins and youths carried the sweet fruit in baskets.

In the midst of them a boy played pleasantly on a shrill lyre,

while they followed, beating the ground in a joyful dance.

- **corn**: Corn is the second agricultural field, where cutting, binding, and feeding are ordered.
- **reapers**: The laborers gathering the thick stalks of deep corn.
- **king**: The king stands in silence and rejoices over ordered harvest.
- **oak**: The oak marks the place where harvest becomes sacrificial meal and communal feeding.
- **vineyard**: A beautiful trench-bound plot heavy with dark, sweet grapes.
- **gold**: The divine, incorruptible metal used to cast everything from fields to gods.

## The Pasture

# The Herd of Oxen

On it he also fashioned a herd of lofty-horned oxen;

but the oxen were made of gold and tin;

and lowing, they rushed from the stall to the pasture,

beside a murmuring river, along the waving reed.

Four golden herdsmen accompanied them, and nine dogs followed.

---

## The Lion Attack

But two terrible lions among the foremost oxen seized a bull,

and he, dragging, roared loudly; but the dogs ran up.

The lions indeed, having torn the hide of the huge ox,

were devouring the entrails and the black blood;

but the shepherds vainly pressed upon them, urging the dogs;

for they refused to bite the lions, but stood very near, barking.

On it also the illustrious Vulcan formed a large pasture,

a beautiful grove for the white sheep, with covered sheds.

- **lions**: Fearsome predators that rip into the golden herd despite the barking dogs.

## The Dancing Floor

# The Dancing Floor

On it also the illustrious Vulcan embellished a dance,

like unto that which in wide Gnossus Daedalus once composed

for the fair-haired Ariadne.

There the youths and the alluring virgins danced,

having their hands on each other's wrists.

Of these, the virgins had on fine linen robes,

but the youths were dressed in well-woven tunics.

These also had beautiful garlands,

and those had golden swords suspended from silver belts.

---

## The Performers

At one time they would move very lightly with skilled feet,

as when a potter makes trial of a wheel fitted to his hands;

and at another time they would move towards each other.

A large crowd stood around the enjoyable dance, delighted.

---

## The Border

But in it he placed the vast strength of the river Oceanus,

around the extreme border of the well-formed shield.

- **dance**: The intricate, spinning movements of youths and maidens in celebration.
- **ocean**: The great river that bounds the cosmos and the edges of the shield.
- **virgins**: The alluring young women carrying baskets and moving lightly in the dance.

## The Dance of Daedalus and the Border of Oceanus

# The Dance of Daedalus and the Border of Oceanus

On it also the illustrious Vulcan embellished a dance.

It was like the dance in wide Gnossus that Daedalus composed for Ariadne.

There youths and alluring virgins danced with hands on each other's wrists.

The virgins had fine linen robes, and the youths wore well-woven tunics.

The youths shone slightly with oil and carried golden swords from silver belts.

At one time they moved lightly with skilled feet, like a potter testing a wheel.

At another time they moved in series towards each other.

A large crowd stood around the enjoyable dance, delighted.

Among them two tumblers, beginning their song, spun round in the midst.

---

In it he placed the vast strength of the river Oceanus.

Oceanus ran around the extreme border of the well-formed shield.

The shield closes where it began: circle, silver belt, dance, river, heaven, sea.

### Links

- **Oceanus**: Oceanus is the enclosing river, the final border that surrounds the whole shield-world.
- **tumblers**: The tumblers spin in the midst, turning the final scene into embodied rotation.
